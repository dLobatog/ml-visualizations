Watch gradient descent find the minimum of a loss function in real time
This is a bird's-eye view of the Rosenbrock function: f(x,y) = (1−x)² + 10·(y−x²)². You're looking down at a landscape where bright = low loss (the valley floor) and dark = high loss (steep walls). The loss equals zero only at (1, 1) — the optimizer's target. This is just 2 parameters — real networks have millions, making the landscape vastly harder to navigate.
