Recommended Routes

Start with a path, not a pile.

The library is broad enough to browse, but it lands better when the chapters feel like routes. These are the reading orders I’d actually recommend for interview prep, teaching, and refreshers.

Interview Core

Foundations → evaluation → practical modeling

If you want the shortest route to sounding sharp in ML interviews, start with probability and optimization, then move into data decisions, metrics, and the two big model families people ask about most.

01 Fundamentals 08 Data & Features 07 Metrics 12 GBDTs 02 Deep Learning
Modern ML

Retrieval, ranking, LLMs, and generative systems

This route is for current production ML: recommenders, search, transformers, retrieval pipelines, and the generative stack that sits on top of them.

04 Recsys & Search 03 LLMs 09 Generative 11 Specialized
Systems

How ML actually survives production

Use this route if the conversation is drifting toward architecture, experimentation, monitoring, distributed training, latency, and model lifecycle tradeoffs.

05 System Design 06 Training At Scale 07 Metrics 08 Data & Features
From Scratch

Build the mechanics before the abstractions

If your goal is deep intuition instead of fast coverage, this is the route to follow. It starts with gradients and ends with full transformer and alignment pipelines.
